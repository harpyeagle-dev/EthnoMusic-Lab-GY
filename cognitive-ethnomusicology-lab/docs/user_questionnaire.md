# Participant Questionnaire

Use this instrument after a session (recording, rehearsal, interview, or listening task). It is designed for cognitive ethnomusicology contexts and can be adapted to local language and consent conditions.

## Administration Notes

- Confirm informed consent before starting.
- Read each item aloud if literacy or language access requires it.
- For Likert items, use a 1-5 scale unless otherwise noted.
- Record contextual notes for culturally specific terms (for example, local genre names, ritual terms, kin terms).

## Section B: Engagement and Experience

Rate each statement from 1 (Strongly Disagree) to 5 (Strongly Agree).

1. I felt highly engaged during this musical activity.
2. I could anticipate key rhythmic changes as they happened.
3. My body movements felt synchronized with the music.
4. I felt socially connected to other participants.
5. I experienced strong emotional response during the session.
6. The musical structure felt familiar to me.
7. The session reflected important community values.

## Section C: Rhythm and Coordination Perception

1. How easy was it to keep time with the group? (1 Very difficult - 5 Very easy)
2. Did tempo feel stable throughout the session? (1 Not stable - 5 Very stable)
3. Which cues helped you stay coordinated? (select all that apply)
   - Lead performer
   - Drums/percussion
   - Clapping/body movement
   - Vocal phrasing
   - Audience responses
   - Other:
4. At what moments did synchronization become stronger or weaker? (free text)

## Section D: Meaning and Cultural Interpretation

1. What did this musical event mean to you personally? (free text)
2. What did this musical event mean for your community? (free text)
3. Were there any specific symbols, stories, or traditions represented? (free text)
4. Were any parts sensitive, restricted, or not for public sharing? (Yes/No; explain)

## Section E: Memory and Attention

1. Which moments do you remember most clearly and why? (free text)
2. Were there moments where your attention shifted? What triggered that shift? (free text)
3. Did participation feel mentally demanding? (1 Not demanding - 5 Very demanding)
4. Did participation feel physically demanding? (1 Not demanding - 5 Very demanding)

## Section F: Consent and Data Use Check

1. Do you allow this recording/summary to be used for research analysis? (Yes/No)
2. Do you allow excerpts in public-facing educational outputs? (Yes/No)
3. Do you require any restrictions on names, places, or segments? (free text)
4. Preferred attribution:
   - Full name
   - Community role only
   - Anonymous

## Section G: Follow-up

1. Is there anything important we did not ask? (free text)
2. Would you like to review findings later? (Yes/No)

## Scoring and Coding Suggestions

- Create composite scores if useful:
  - `engagement_score`: mean of Section B items 1, 3, 5.
  - `coordination_score`: mean of Section B item 2 and Section C items 1, 2.
  - `social_connection_score`: Section B item 4.
- Preserve open-text responses for thematic coding in transcript workflows.
- Flag any restricted-content responses for protected handling in archives and exports.
