# Ethnomusic Lab GY Curriculum Sequence

This sequence orders the four community strands into a progression for teaching and facilitation.

## Stage 1: Kabakaburi

### Kabakaburi Living Heritage Archive (Kabakaburi)
- Tradition: Community song and dance practice
- Key learning objectives:
  - Document traditional songs, recordings, performances, and sonic heritage
- Core activities:
  - Explore traditional songs, recordings, performances, and sonic heritage
- Source: https://digitalheritagegy.com/s/s/kabakaburi/page/home

### Kamarang Keng: Digital Heritage Archive (Kabakaburi)
- Tradition: Ceremonial and social music
- Key learning objectives:
  - Identify key musical traits documented in this community source
  - Connect musical practice to local cultural context
- Core activities:
  - Explore close reading of community source materials
  - Explore group rhythm and listening reflection activity
- Source: https://digitalheritagegy.com/s/s/kamarang/page/home

### Waramadong Heritage Centre (Kabakaburi)
- Tradition: Intergenerational rhythm transmission
- Key learning objectives:
  - Identify key musical traits documented in this community source
  - Connect musical practice to local cultural context
- Core activities:
  - Explore stories, songs, traditions and community knowledge
  - Listen to elders, youth and community perspectives
- Source: https://digitalheritagegy.com/s/s/waramadong/page/home

### Santa Rosa Cultural Archive (Kabakaburi)
- Tradition: Festival and village performance traditions
- Key learning objectives:
  - Document indigenous music traditions and performance
  - Document cultural practices and traditional knowledge
- Core activities:
  - Explore close reading of community source materials
  - Explore group rhythm and listening reflection activity
- Source: https://digitalheritagegy.com/s/s/santa-rosa/page/welcome
